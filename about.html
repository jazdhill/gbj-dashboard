A good job is getting harder to reach

This resource tracks a single, stubborn question: can you still reach the middle class in America, and does the answer depend on a degree you may not have, or on race and gender you can't change?

Drawing on Current Population Survey data going back to 1976, it maps which jobs pay enough to land a worker in the middle class, which of those are open to people without a college degree, and who actually holds them. The headline finding is stark: fewer than 1 in 100 workers hold a job that is both good and reachable without a degree, and for Black workers that share is smaller still.

It is built to be a standing reference, not a one-off chart. Reporters, students, and anyone weighing their own options can use it to answer real questions about work, education, and opportunity.
