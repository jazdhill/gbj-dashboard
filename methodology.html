How we measure a good job

Three definitions do most of the work in this dashboard. Here's what each one means and where the numbers come from.

What counts as a "good job"

A job is counted as good if its typical pay clears a middle-class threshold, set at the U.S. median household income. In the current version that threshold is $83,730. You can change the threshold and the geographic scope inside the dashboard to see how the picture shifts.

"College-optional" versus "college-required"

College-optional jobs are occupations a worker can enter without a bachelor's degree. College-required jobs typically expect one. Separating the two is what lets the tool ask whether a good job is still reachable without a degree, and track how that has changed.

Where the data comes from

The underlying figures draw on the Current Population Survey, accessed through IPUMS, covering 1976 through 2025 at both the national and California level. Occupation-level income, education, demographics, union coverage, health coverage, and projected growth come from these public sources.

A note on small samples

Some occupations are held by relatively few survey respondents. Where a demographic estimate rests on a small sample, the dashboard flags it so those numbers are read with appropriate caution rather than treated as precise.
